OneTalk Messenger
Chat Bubbles

OneTalk optimizes the voice recognition technology to make mobile communication faster & “lazier”. Chat Bubbles save time for users and makes replying messages much easier.

  • Role：Product Management | UX Design | UI Design |
  • Type：Android Application
  • Award：Google Play The Most Innovative App of 2017
  • Try it on Google Play Store



Background

As a startup company, we started this idea from our own needs. “Too lazy to type on the phone.” “Sometimes I want to keep my hands-free”. This is where we start to do the work for this application.

Early Stage - Improve Current Voice Messaging Experience

At the very beginning, we applied current speech recognition services, and try to optimize the usability while sending voice messages. People send message in two ways. One is only text and if they are not able to type, they send voice message. However, sender and receiver won’t be at the same environment. They might not be able to type by hand or hear your voice on speaker. So why not put both text and voice together? To hear the voice is also better to understand the emotion of a person. Eliminate the misunderstanding on message.

Transcribe voice to text completes the messaging experience.
We also add some features to make sending messaages easier.

Expand Voice Typing Use Case

From one of our user feedbacks on Google Play, he asked if he can use our service while leaving OneTalk, such as gaming. So we start to move the experience outside the app. So the Chat Bubbles feature started from here.

Bring Instant Messengers All Together with OneTalk

People got all different instant messengers on their phone. It’s a hassle to reply messages by switching between different messaging apps. We found a solution to reply all incoming messages by OneTalk Chat Bubbles for major IM. This made Chat Bubbles much more useful and it also help us to promote our app when replying.

Iterations

After main feature had been locked down, we focuesed on optimize the usability of Chat Bubbles. We tracked Google Anaylisis data to iterate the hierachy of function. We also added canned response to let users reply messages even faster.

OneTalk Bot

OneTalk Bot includes three different chatbots service. Our goal is to explore various service possibilities through conversational user interface, provide delightful and friendly experience for engaging more of our users.

  • Role：UX Design | UI Design |
  • Type：Android & iOS Application
  • Award：Google Play The Most Innovative App of 2017
  • Try it on Google Play Store

Mr.Otter

When users first entering OneTalk, they will meet Mr. Otter. The purpose of this chatbot is to provide tutorials and customer supports. Users can talk to Mr.Otter and test the voice recognition. We also used Wizard of Oz method to chat with users and learn from users.

Insights from the chat Log

Looking at chat log can better understand our users. It also helped us design better interaction through conversational user interface.

Say hi to your users

To make a good first impression with users, say Hi to users is an critical step. Randomized the ways to say Hi can also engage them more. Because most users like to keep poking the bot and just for fun.

Always response the user

Users hate to be ignored. As long as users get response, it’s better than nothing. Even though the bot cannot satisfy the user needs, say sorry or provide further contact info will be helpful.

Bot abuse

Users say something bad to the bot is pretty common. List out the bad words and teach the bot to reply politely can alleviate the negative interaction.

Valuable thoughts from Target Users

Most of our target users are teenagers, it was hard to know their thoughts without talking to them privately. We used the channel from Mr.Otter to have a deeper interview with them. We’ve learned how they interact with friends by messaging, popular YouTuber among their generations and the most used social media. All these information helped us to promote OneTalk on the marketing.


Magic DJ

Since people listen and watch music video a lot on YouTube nowaday, we built a MagicDJ for optimizing experience with YouTube. After requesting music from MagicDJ, it pop-up a floating windows on the screen(Android phone) even you leave OneTalk app. MagicDJ also recommends playlist proactively through conversational user interface. This feature was highly praised from our users, however, we want to try more possibility. Therefore, Music Remote feature was borned.

MagicDJ + ChatBubbles + AI = Music Remote

We have built MagicDJ and Chat Bubbles on OneTalk, what if we can send commands from all different instant messengers to control Magic DJ?

Why Music Remote
  • 1. Music Remote can transform your (spare) phone into a smart speaker.
  • 2. People can remote control music at a party from their instant messengers without installing OneTalk.
  • 3. You can save money from buying other voice control device such as Echo.
  • 4. MagicDJ learns from your commands and preferences.

ASUS ZenUI - ZenFone Multimedia Apps

ZenUI is the system user interface designed specifically for ZenFone. Including various essential multimedia apps for ZenFone users to experience consistent and seamless services.

  • Role: Planning | Researching | Wireframing | Prototyping | Testing
  • Type: ZenFone system applications | Android applications
  • Projects: MiniMovie | Music | Gallery | Voice Recording | FM Radio | System Settings

Background

ZenUI is the system user interface designed for ZenFone. Before ZenFone1 was borned, ASUS design center decided to create their own user interface since there was no standard design guideline for android phone back in 2013. I was involved in the this plan and focusing on planning and designing the essential multimedia applications for ZenFone.

Challanges

Timing: Before ZenFone launched, there was only two to three months for building new application from zero to release.

Integration: We hoped to support most cloud storage services, home storage services (such as NAS) to make people easily access their multimedia files. Supporting arious streaming technology was also important for multimedia apps. Integrating these features in the user flow and make the users feel non-hessle was the main design goal.

Communication: Not like designing an independent application, all the interactions of ZenFone apps needs to be consistent. Different app designers will need to cooperate tightly and efficiently to make this happened.

Harman Kardon Remote

Harman Kardon remote is a mobile application to control Harman Kardon audio products. Main features including "Device Control" , "Music Streaming" and "Music Player".

  • Role: UX/ UI Design
  • Type：iOS/Android Application
  • Try it on Google Play Store

Background

Harman Kardon manufactures home audio system, back in 2012, since smart phone had become more popular on the market, supporting remote control and streaming services from mobile phone was a must. Harman Kardon UI/UX design team started to create this application to control all existing products in the app.

Challenge

High complexity: Desingers needed to dive deeply for the technology of all audio products, including researching and defining interactions for more than 10 Harman Kardon products.
Integration: Other than remote control, also designed a full funtion music player integrating into the application. How to integrate all different setup process and make the navigation easy to understand was a challange.
