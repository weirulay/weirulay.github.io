OneTalk Messenger-Chat Bubbles

OneTalk optimizes the voice recognition technology to make mobile communication faster, simpler, and more intuitive. The Chat Bubbles feature saves time for users and makes replying to messages simpler and easier.

  • Role: UX Design | UI Design
  • Type: Android Application
  • Award: Google Play Most Innovative App of 2017
  • Try it on Google Play Store



Background

As a startup company, we built this idea from our own needs. “Too lazy to type on the phone.” “Sometimes I want to keep my hands-free”. These daily scenarios inspired us to create the voice-based messenger, OneTalk.

Stage One - Improve current voice messaging experience

In the beginning, we applied current speech-recognition services and try to improve the usability for sending voice messages. People send message in two ways: text or voice message. However, senders and receivers aren’t necessarily in compatible environments. They might not be able to type by hand or hear your voice on speaker. What if we could display both text and voice together? Be able to hear the voice can also better to understand the emotion of a person. It can eliminate the misunderstanding from text-only messages.

Automatic voice-to-text transcription completes the messaging experience.
Advanced features to fit various occasions.

Stage Two - Expand Voice Typing Use Case

One user on Google Play suggested expanding our service for uses outside OneTalk, such as gaming. So we moved the experience beyond the app. This is how the Chat Bubbles feature started.

Stage Three - Merge Instant-Messaging Services into OneTalk

People have many different instant messengers on their phones. Having to switch between apps to reply to messages is a hassle. We created a solution to reply to all incoming messages with OneTalk Chat Bubbles for leading instant-messaging platforms, such as SMS, Facebook Messenger, WhatsApp, and others. This made the Chat Bubbles feature much more useful and it also helped us to promote the app every time a user replies to a message.

Iterations

After the main features were successfully integrated, we focused on optimizing the usability of the Chat Bubbles feature. Using Google Analytics data, we were able to determine which functions users tended to favor. We also added pre-written messages to let users reply with common responses even more quickly.

OneTalk Bot

OneTalk Bot includes a service, featuring three unique chatbots. Our goal was to explore various service possibilities through conversational user interface and to provide a delightful, friendly experience to attract more users and to engage them more deeply and enjoyably.

  • Role：UX Design | UI Design |
  • Type：Android and iOS Application
  • Award：Google Play Most Innovative App of 2017
  • Try it on Google Play Store

Mr.Otter

When users first enter OneTalk, they meet Mr. Otter. The purpose of the Mr. Otter chatbot is to provide tutorials and customer support. Users can talk to Mr. Otter and test the voice recognition. We also used Wizard of Oz method to chat with users and learn from them.

Insights from the chat Log

Looking at the chat log helped us better understand our users. It also helped us design better interaction through conversational user interface.

Say hi to your users

To make a good first impression with users, saying “Hi” to users is an critical step. Randomized ways to say “Hi” have been proven to . We found many users enjoyed clicking on the bot just for fun.

Always response the user

Users hate to be ignored. Even if the bot cannot satisfy everything a user might need, having it say “sorry” or provide further contact info is still helpful.

Bot abuse

It’s common for users to swear at, curse, or otherwise verbally abuse bots. Programming words and phrases and teaching the bot to reply politely can alleviate negative user interactions.

Valuable thoughts from target users

Although our design team is in our 30s, most of our target users are teenagers, and it was hard to know their thoughts without speaking to them directly. We used the Mr. Otter channel to conduct deeper interviews with them. We learned how they interact with friends by messaging, popular YouTube channels among their peers, and the most used social media. This data helped us to better promote OneTalk on the marketing side.


Magic DJ

Given the rise of music-video streaming on YouTube, we decided to build MagicDJ for optimizing experience on the site. After requesting music from MagicDJ, a pop-up floating windows on the screen (Android phone-only), even you leave the OneTalk app. MagicDJ also recommends playlists proactively through conversational user interface. This feature was highly praised from our users. However, we wanted to explore more possibilities. This was where the idea for the Music Remote feature originated.

MagicDJ + ChatBubbles + AI = Music Remote

Having built MagicDJ and Chat Bubbles on OneTalk, we wondered: What if you could send commands from instant messengers to control Magic DJ?

Why Music Remote
  • 1. Music Remote transforms your (spare) phone into a smart speaker.
  • 2. Friends are able to remotely control music at a party from their instant messengers without installing OneTalk.
  • 3. Music Remote saves you money by removing the need to purchase additional voice-control devices, such as Echo.
  • 4. MagicDJ learns from your commands and preferences.



ASUS ZenUI - ZenFone Apps

ZenUI is the user interface designed specifically for ASUS ZenFone. It includes various multimedia apps for ZenFone users to experience a wide range of consistent, seamless services.

  • Role: Planning | Researching | Wireframing | Prototyping | Testing
  • Type: ZenFone system applications | Android applications
  • Projects: MiniMovie | Music | Gallery | Voice Recording | FM Radio | System Settings
  • More about ASUS ZenUI
  • More about ASUS ZenFone

Background

ZenUI is the user interface designed for ZenFone. Before the 2014 release of ZenFone1, ASUS design center needed to create their own user interface. At the time, the design guidelines for Android phones were incomplete. I was involved in creating the ZenUI user interface, and focused on planning and designing the essential multimedia application for ZenFone.

Challenges

Timing: Our team was under a tight deadline—two to three months—for building the new application from scratch.

Integration: We wanted to support most cloud-storage services and, home-storage services (such as NAS) so that users could easily access their multimedia files. We also want to support various streaming technologies. Our main design goal was to integrate these features into the user flow and make users feel comfortable.

Communication: Unlike third-party applications, all ZenFone app interactions need to be consistent and unified. Designers on ZenFone apps needed to cooperate closely and effectively to achieve our goal.

KikaGo

In-car voice control solution creates a safer, more convenient and effortless way to communicate on the road.

  • Role: UX designer
  • Type: Voice User Interface for Automobile
  • Award: CES 2018 Innovation Award Winner of In-Vehicle Audio/Video, Tech for a Better World, Software and Mobile Apps, and Smart Home
  • KikaGo Official Home Page
  • KikaGo 2018 CES Interview


Background

In the loud environments, such as inside a moving vehicle, a regular phone's microphone is less effective for voice control. Kika developed a noise-cancellation microphone built into a USB-charging cable to solve this problem. With Kika's cutting-edge AI engine, the KikaGo application created a truly hands-free means for communications inside a car environment that is also elegant and simple.

Challenges

At the time of KikaGo’s development, there were no hands-free, in-car communications on the market. Unlike existing voice-assisted agents like Siri, KikaGo was designed specifically for voice interaction and hands-free communication so that users would be able to interact without seeing or touching the screen. Because of this, the design flow would need to be more abstract and difficult than the conventional user interface, which includes a visual component, and designers would need to spend more time testing the voice interaction, as opposed to the visual interaction design.

Harman Kardon Remote

Harman Kardon Remote is a mobile application for controlling Harman Kardon audio products. The main features included device control, music streaming, and music player.

  • Role: UX/ UI Design
  • Type：iOS/Android Application

Background

Harman Kardon is a home audio system manufacturer. With the rise of smartphones, mobile-phone support for remote control and streaming services had become a must. In 2012, the Harman Kardon UI/UX design team began developing this application to give users a more simpler, more intuitive, and technologically integrated smarter way to interact with audio products.

Challenges

Because of complex and meticulous nature of the project, detailed knowledge and execution was required. Designers researched and defined interactions for over ten Harmon Kardon products, including the BDS home-audio system and TK, in order to replicate and integrate these functions for mobile-phone remote control.

In addition to the remote control, designers also develop a fully functioning music player integrating into the app. Another challenge was how we could implement a wide variety of setup processes while simultaneously improving on making the navigation, making the end result more intuitive, elegant, and easy to understand.
